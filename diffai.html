Different Types of AI

Introduction

Artificial Intelligence (AI) can be broadly categorized into various types based on capabilities and functionalities. Understanding these categories helps us grasp the scope and potential of AI technologies.

Narrow AI

Narrow AI, also known as Weak AI, is designed to perform specific tasks. Examples include voice assistants like Siri and Alexa, and recommendation systems used by Netflix and Amazon.

General AI

General AI, or Strong AI, aims to possess cognitive abilities comparable to humans. It can understand, learn, and apply knowledge across a wide range of tasks.

Super AI

Super AI surpasses human intelligence and can perform any cognitive task better than a human. It remains a theoretical concept and is the subject of much speculation and debate.

Reactive Machines

Reactive Machines are a basic form of AI that can perform specific tasks but lack memory-based functionality. IBM's Deep Blue chess-playing computer is an example.

Limited Memory

Limited Memory AI systems can use past experiences to inform future decisions. Self-driving cars are an example of this type of AI.

Theory of Mind

Theory of Mind AI is still in developmental stages and aims to understand human emotions, beliefs, and interactions. This could enable more natural and effective human-AI interactions in the future.

Self-Aware AI

Self-Aware AI is the most advanced type of AI, which has its own consciousness and self-awareness. This type of AI is purely hypothetical at this stage.
